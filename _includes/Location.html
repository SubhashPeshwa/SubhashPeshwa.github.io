Tourist Attractions

The Chail Palace was built as summer retreat by the Maharaja of Patiala during the British Raj. The cricket ground and a polo ground which is there at an altitude of 2,250 m was owned by erstwhile royal family of Patiala. It is the world's highest cricket ground.
Chail -50KM
Protected by the government, Glen forest is an oldest picnic spot of the city with chadwick falls surrounded by pine and deodar trees.
Glen Forest -3.6KM
An ancient temple with 108 feet high idol,Jakhu is believed to hold the footprints of Lord Hanuman.
Jhaku Temple -16.5KM
The Mall Road is Shimla's main street that enfolds many hotels, restaurants, clubs, bars, banks, shops, offices, post offices and tourist offices. One can enjoy his favourite icecream, walking down the street.
Mall Road -5KM
Lakkar bazar is a marketplace that offers wooden articles with Shimla's signature embeded on them.
Lakkar Bazaar -6KM
The Rashtrapati Niwas aka Viceregal Lodge was formerly the residence of the British Viceroy of India. It houses some of the most ancient articles and photographs going back to the times of the British rule in India.
Viceroy Regal Lodge -3.6KM
The name Kufri is derived from the word kufr meaning "lake" in the local language. Kufri is famous for Himalayan wild life zoo, Fun world amusement park and Chini Bunglow.
Kuffri -22KM
Nestled between the beautiful lofty ranges of the Himalayas, lies the Naldehra hill station. Its 18 - hole golf course is considered to be the most challenging golf course because of its 2044 meters altitude.
Naldehra -20KM
